The Rise of Cryptocurrencies

The unprecedented rise in cryptocurrencies, powered by the revolutionary Blockchain technology, has led to speculation that cryptocurrencies may replace gold as the globally accepted medium of exchange and store of value. We strongly believe such speculation to be short-sighted as gold has properties that make it an ideal store of value. Instead, we believe that physical gold and cryptocurrencies are complementary. Combined they provide a unique value proposition which can transform the financial market by creating gold-backed digital currencies.

Why Gold and Cryptocurrenices are Complementary

Gold has many unique features that differentiate it from cryptocurrencies, which include, but are not limited to: lower volatility, intrinsic value (i.e. jewellery and technology), limited supply, regulatory stability and lower risk of theft (i.e. cannot be hacked). Accordingly, we believe that gold will continue to excel as the preferred store of value. Instead of competing with one another, gold and cryptocurrencies are complementary. Cryptocurrency is not the new form of money, it is the new medium for trading money. Cryptocurrencies and gold combine to form an extremely robust platform for trading digital tokens that benefits from combining the Blockchain technology with gold’s unique attributes that makes it the preferred store of value.

The Opportunity

Metals House Inc. (“Metals House”), a global physical precious metals trading firm, is uniquely qualified to take advantage of the complementary nature of gold and cryptocurrencies by issuing its gold-backed Security Token Offering. Metals House’s business model is ideal as it does not earn revenues by speculating on gold market price fluctuations. With its stringent risk management policies, Metals House earns profits by arbitraging the pricing spreads between physical precious metals as commodities and physical precious metals as currencies. We believe that Metals House has a bright future due to the projected supply/demand imbalance. New gold supply is projected to decline as current reserves are depleted and costs increase to reach less accessible gold reserves. Demand will increase as global investors with increasing wealth view gold as a strategic asset that increases risk adjusted returns. Metals House’s competitive advantages combined with the demand/supply imbalance provides the ideal opportunity to take advantage of market structural gaps which include decentralized and undercapitalized competitors, onerous regulatory requirements and gold sourcing.
